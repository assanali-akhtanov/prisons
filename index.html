The U.S. makes up only 5% of the global population but accounts for nearly 25% of the world's prison population. This staggering disparity reveals the immense scale of the country's reliance on incarceration as a solution to societal issues.

More Americans are incarcerated today than there have been Americans killed in all of the wars in all of history combined.

Knowing all of this forces us to ask an uncomfortable question.

What if a better world were possible?

A better world is closer than you may think. It's something we can accomplish in our lifetime.

The first thing you need to understand is that mass incarceration is not a solution to safety problems.

How do we fix it then?

1) Sentencing Reform: Implementing policies that reduce mandatory minimum sentences, especially for non-violent offenses, can significantly decrease the prison population. Eliminating harsh sentencing laws like the "three strikes" rule and offering more opportunities for parole would allow individuals to avoid unnecessarily long prison terms.

2) Decriminalization of Non-Violent Offenses: Decriminalizing low-level, non-violent crimes such as drug possession and certain traffic violations could prevent millions from entering the prison system. Shifting the focus from punishment to treatment, such as offering rehabilitation for substance abuse, can address underlying issues without incarceration.

3) Investment in Education and Job Training Programs: By investing in education, vocational training, and job placement programs for at-risk communities, the root causes of crime—such as poverty and lack of opportunity—can be addressed. Preventative measures that promote economic stability reduce the likelihood of individuals entering the criminal justice system in the first place.

4) Expansion of Diversion Programs and Restorative Justice: Programs that offer alternatives to incarceration, such as drug courts, mental health courts, and restorative justice initiatives, can help individuals avoid prison while addressing the root causes of their behavior. These programs promote accountability, healing, and community reintegration rather than punishment.

5) Improving Public Defense and Legal Support: Strengthening the public defender system by providing more funding, resources, and legal assistance can ensure that defendants receive a fair trial and are not coerced into plea deals. Better legal representation can prevent unnecessary imprisonment and reduce wrongful convictions.

We need to remember that a better world is possible
